Women in Humanities

Let's meet some inspiring women in Geography, History, Politics and Law!

Greta Thunberg

Greta Thunberg is an environmental activist. At the age of 15, Greta missed school to sit outside the Swedish parliament with a sign which read ‘SCHOOL STRIKE FOR CLIMATE’, her aim was to encourage politicians to take more action against climate change. Since then, tens of thousands of students from around the world have joined her #FridaysforFuture strikes – skipping school on Fridays to protest against climate change.

Jacinda Ardern

Jacinda Ardern is the prime minister of New Zealand. Jacinda is a passionate advocate for women's rights and famously had a child during her leadership, proving that women can have children whilst maintaining a successful career.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafza is a pakistani education activist. She is known for her activism for girls' and women's rights, especially for being allowed to go to school. In 2013, she was nominated for the 2013 Nobel Peace Prize.

Mhairi Black

Mhairi is a British politician. In 2015, she became the youngest MP to be elected in 350 years. Her first speech at the House of Commons has been viewed online nearly 11 million times.

Professor Kate Williams

Kate Williams is a history professor at the University of Reading. She also works as a TV presenter and has appeared on shows such as the The Great British Bake Off and documentaries about Queen Victoria and Sherlock Holmes.
